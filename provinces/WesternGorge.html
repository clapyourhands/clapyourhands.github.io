Western Gorge

The Vast Divide

The Western Gorge Province is largely uninhabited, save for the small, sleepy town of Tellston (not shown) and the Kotami peoples of the northeastern canyons. The province in ancient times used to be home to the illustrious Tellari civilization, whose ruins still dot the Tellari Desert. Much of Tellston's economy is based upon tourism to these ruins as well as the selling of artifacts to tourists and wealthy aristocrats. The Tellari built the massive Tellari Gate to keep out foreign invaders; however, the only people living on the other side of the gate today are the peaceful Kotami. The Kotami carve out homes within the deep canyons of Sundering Gorge. They are self-isolating and have an elaborate social stratification system in which the upper-class literally dwell at the higher, sunlit altitudes while the poor survive in the darker depths. Thanks to their unique dwellings, Sakhin raiders to their southeast cannot do much harm to them while mounted on their horses.







Tellari Ruins

Little is known of the Tellari civilization, and what has been discovered has largely been already ransacked by tomb raiders. The Sankun Stele is the only major, intact artifact that has been uncovered to date.







Sundering Gorge

No geographical feature dominates the continent more than Sundering Gorge. Splitting the land in two, the chasm separates east from west as well as any ocean.







Kotami Canyons

The Kotami are the only people that can truly call the Sundering Gorge home. Trading with merchants from Wickhamford and Kindleton, they make their livings primarily as skilled craftsmen.
