Wickhamford

Palace of Saints

Wickhamford is located on the central western coast of Astalon. Once the kingdom's capital, it still maintains much of its original glory. Wickhamford is famous across the kingdom for its white marble Saints' Quarter, home to the Oracle of Rue. A statue of the kingdom's unifier Dolomir the Great, stands in its center, honoring the man who spread her word across the land. The province itself is the site of a famous Astaloni myth, and pilgrims journey from all over the region to visit these sites. On Carran Point, the town of Carran is locally famous for its fresh seafood and kelp. In the north, Orkenny is home to the Chungo orc tribe, who carry Fjerdan textiles and lumber throughout west and central Astalon. The Smoky River is notable for its ashy water, filled with soot picked up in the Smokestone Mountains. It is thought that ash from Bael's Crater (not shown) is responsible for this residue.







Saint's Quarter

Located in the heart of Wickhamford, the Saint's Quarter is the largest and more important Ruithan holy site. The marble Saint's Basilica stands tall in its center, home to the Oracle of Rue. Pilgrims journey from around the continent to seek wisdom and healing from the Oracle.







Rue's Peak

A tall mountain standing alone on the plains of eastern Astalon, Rue's Peak is said to be where the goddess Rue ascended after becoming mortal and freeing the people from the grips of the Demon King. Few have successfully summited this forbidding spire. Some say that at night, one can reach the stars from the top of the peak.







Isles of Kells

Seldom discussed and even more seldomly visited, the Isles of Kells are a desolate pair of islets off the east coast of Astalon. Said to be cleaved in two by the Demon King, no plants grow there, and the stone itself is a pallid gray, as if the life has been drained from it entirely.
